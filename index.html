About Me

I'm currently pursuing an MPA in Data Science for Public Policy at the London School of Economics as a David and Duncan Clark Scholar, one of only 2 recipients from Asia.

With a background in Economics and Mathematics from LUMS Pakistan, I've spent the last few years working at the intersection of data science, policy research, and international development.

I'm passionate about leveraging data-driven insights to solve complex policy challenges and create social impact. My work spans from consulting with USAID on Pakistan-US trade to researching gender discrimination in online marketplaces.

I'm also the founder of PeerPathway, a social enterprise connecting students with mentors for graduate studies guidance.
